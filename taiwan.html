Go Website

Go is a strategic board game played on a grid-based board with black and white stones. It originated in China and was historically known by various names such as "Yi" (弈), "Qi" (碁), and "Shou Tan" (手談). Go is also one of the Four Arts of ancient Chinese scholars, alongside the guqin (琴), calligraphy (書), and painting (畫).

About Haifeng

In 1997, Mr. Lin Wenbo, driven by his love for Go, decided to donate 30 million yuan to establish a foundation aimed at promoting Go. After consulting with National Go Master Lin Haifeng, he received full support and was promised to serve as the first chairman. The "Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation" was thus established, launching the cause of promoting Go. Due to the significant investment of both money and manpower required for organizing professional and amateur competitions, the Haifeng Foundation has been generously funded by Chairman Lin Wenbo over the years, leading to rapid growth. However, the foundation still faced difficulties in hosting professional tournaments. As a result, organizational adjustments were necessary. Chairman Lin decided to merge the "Haifeng Cultural and Educational Foundation" with the "Pearson Cultural and Educational Foundation" to create synergies greater than the sum of their parts.

The Pearson Cultural and Educational Foundation, another foundation funded by Chairman Lin Wenbo and his family, has long been dedicated to special education. As its financial resources are more abundant than those of the Haifeng Foundation, after the merger, the Haifeng Foundation ceased its operations and handed over the responsibility of promoting the Go movement to the "Pearson Cultural and Educational Foundation." The original staff of the Haifeng Foundation was restructured and reorganized into the "Haifeng Go Institute" to continue their efforts in promoting Go on the front lines.
